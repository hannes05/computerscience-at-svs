1. What is Personal Data?

Personal data is any information that is linked to a specific person. This could include your name, address, date of birth, email, phone number, or even your location.

  • Examples of personal data: First name, mobile number, date of last doctor visit
  • Not personal data: The postcode of a school, number of students in a school, name of a local beach

💡 Remember: If information can be linked to you specifically, it's personal data!

2. Who Holds Personal Data About You?

Many organizations hold personal data about you and your family. Here are some examples:

Organization Data They Hold
Your School Name, address, attendance, grades, medical information
Email Provider Email address, contacts, messages, IP address
Online Shops Name, address, payment details, purchase history
Banks Account details, transactions, savings, loans
Social Media Profile information, photos, location, browsing habits
Mobile Phone Companies Phone number, usage data, location tracking

3. The Data Protection Act (2018) & GDPR

The Data Protection Act (2018) is UK law that protects your personal data. It implements GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) rules.

Responsibilities of organizations:

  • 🔍 Tell you what data is collected and how it will be used
  • 📊 Only collect data that is needed
  • ⏱️ Only keep data whilst it is needed
  • 🔒 Hold your data securely

Your rights:

  • ✅ The right to access your personal data
  • ✏️ The right to have inaccurate data corrected
  • 🚫 The right to withdraw consent (for some types of data)

The Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) is responsible for enforcing data protection in the UK and can issue fines to organizations that misuse data.

4. Case Study: British Airways Data Breach

⚠️ Real-world incident:

  • In 2020, British Airways had 380,000-500,000 customer records stolen
  • 75,000 records included names, addresses, credit card numbers and CV2 codes
  • The ICO fined British Airways £20 million for the data breach
  • Customers had money stolen as a result of the breach
🔗 Read more about the BA breach

5. Case Study: Dixons Carphone Data Breach

⚠️ Another major breach:

Date:July 2017 - April 2018
Data stolen:14 million personal records, 5.6 million payment cards
Method:Malicious software installed on 5,390 shop tills
Fine:£500,000
Impact:100 Carphone Warehouse shops closed, company rebranded to Currys in 2021

6. What is Identity Theft?

Identity theft is when criminals find information about you and use it to pretend they are you. This is typically used to commit fraud.

Information used for identity theft includes:

  • Bank statements and bills
  • Passports and driving licenses
  • Name, age, place of birth
  • Credit card details
  • Passwords and PINs
🔗 Learn more about identity theft

7. Protecting Against Identity Theft

Here are some important ways to protect your identity:

  • 🗑️ Shred documents that contain personal information before throwing them away
  • 🔐 Use strong passwords for computers and online services (mix of letters, numbers, symbols)
  • 📱 Limit personal information shared on social media
  • 🔒 Encrypt hard drives and removable storage devices
  • 👀 Be aware of shoulder surfing (people looking over your shoulder)
  • 🚫 Don't click suspicious links in emails or messages

8. What is Shoulder Surfing?

Shoulder surfing is when someone looks over your shoulder to see what you're typing, especially passwords or PINs.

How to protect yourself:

  • Cover the keyboard with your other hand while typing passwords
  • Be aware of your surroundings when entering sensitive information
  • Position yourself so others can't see your screen
  • Use privacy screens on devices

⚠️ Remember: Using information gained from shoulder surfing is illegal under the Computer Misuse Act!

9. Social Media Safety

Smartphones and social media often contain huge amounts of personal data:

  • 📸 Photos revealing where you live or go to school
  • 📍 Location data showing your movements
  • 💬 Messages and conversations
  • 💳 Financial information and passwords
  • 🏥 Medical information
  • 🌐 Web browsing history

Danger: Posting too much information online can help criminals build a profile of you for identity theft or other crimes!
